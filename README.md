## What You're Actually Getting

All plans come with the full standard kit you'd expect — cPanel, free SSL certificates, daily backups stored at two locations, Softaculous (one-click installer for 400+ scripts including WordPress, Joomla, and Drupal), spam protection, and South Africa-based servers. That last bit matters. If your audience is local, hosting your site closer to them means faster load times and better local SEO.

A few things stand out as genuinely above average:

- **Daily backups at two separate locations** — this is more than most budget hosts bother with
- **No server overloading guaranteed** — they explicitly commit to not cramming too many accounts onto a single server
- **Free access to their marketing system** — included with Business, Professional, and Corporate plans. This is an internet marketing company offering their own developed tools for free. Not something you see often.
- **Free website included** — bundled with hosting packages through their sister service, Dynamic Websites

## Who This Is For

The **Personal plan** (R45/month) is fine for a simple brochure site or a personal blog where traffic is modest. The storage is tight at 100 MB though — if you're running a WordPress site with images, you'll bump into that ceiling.

The **Business plan** at R115/month (or R92/month on annual billing) is where it starts making real sense. You get 5 GB of storage, 100 email addresses, free domain, and access to their marketing tools. For a small South African business trying to get online without a lot of faff, this is probably the sweet spot.

The **Professional and Corporate plans** are for agencies or anyone running multiple client sites under one account — 10 and 20 domains respectively, more storage, and room to scale.
